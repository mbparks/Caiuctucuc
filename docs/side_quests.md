# CAIUCTUCUC
## Side Quest Sketches

**Companion to Design Document v0.3.0, Beat Sheet v0.1.0, Map Layout v0.1.0, Keyword Matrix v0.1.0**
Side Quests v0.1.0
Author: M.B. Parks
Date: July 2026

Sixteen side quests plus four Burden quests, hung off the roster's unused secrets and built so each one exercises a specific system. Tags: [v1.0] ships in the MVP, [post] lands after. Nothing here is required for any ending, but several quietly improve the main quest's outcomes, which is the design intent: side content should make the town more solvable, not just longer.

---

## The Road

**1. The Excise Man's Arithmetic** [v1.0]
Hook: dock workers grumble that Pyle's seizure tallies never match what was seized. Beats: shadow one seizure, compare the warehouse count against the courthouse filing (Rood helps at trust). Resolution branches: report him (Town rises, Road burns, Pyle becomes a courtroom enemy), blackmail him (coin now, a lie on the ledger of your own), or partner quietly (Road rises, and Pyle owes you one favor redeemable during Act III's checkpoints). Systems: reputation opposition, the lie mechanic. Reward: coin or a favor flag.

**2. Cave-Aged** [v1.0]
Hook: Marsh needs whiskey moved through the Narrows past the toll and, later, past militia checkpoints. Three escalating runs teaching fords, dark-lantern travel, and chase geography. The third run detours through a cave fork Marsh marks with a nailed horseshoe, which returning players will recognize in Act IV's descent. Systems: hue and cry, mounts, world knowledge as foreshadowing. Reward: Road trusted, the STILL keyword, a standing whiskey discount that is secretly a bribe budget.

**3. No Questions** [v1.0]
Hook: Shanks asks the player to walk beside a night crossing and say nothing, whatever happens. The passengers are a family moving north with papers from Freeman's loft. A rider with a Virginia warrant is watching the landing. Beats: get them from the shore to Bright's departing wagons by any quiet route the player knows; the rider can be misled (TONGUE), decoyed (disguise), or simply outwalked by superior map knowledge. There is no comic beat in this quest and no coin at the end of it. Written with the gravity the subject requires: the family are people, briefly known, and the game lets them be remembered by name. Systems: stealth, routes, trust. Reward: Freeman's PAPERS unlock, Shanks and Freeman to maximum trust, and one line in the epilogue that outweighs most endings.

## The Town

**4. The Magistrate's Ledger** [v1.0]
Hook: Cresap's bribe ledger exists; Peg will sell that fact. Beats: get into his office (invitation, lockpicks, or his clerk's grudge), then choose to copy it, take it, or leave it and remember. The copy is leverage that can force the Act III trial when the elders waver; the theft makes Cresap an enemy with legal reach; the memory is worth trust with Kent later. Systems: the three-route pattern in miniature. Reward: the WARRANT-era leverage flag.

**5. The Sealed Record** [v1.0]
Hook: Kent's chambers hold a record sealed by his own hand. Beats: the game's best lock, or Rood's help, or Kent's own guilt worked patiently through trust. The record is an inquest Kent quashed as a young clerk: a quarry death from decades back, same seam, ruled accident against evidence, because the town's founders demanded it. Kent has spent a career atoning in secret. Resolution: pressure him (compromised judge, pliable but brittle) or absolve him (ally in atonement, incorruptible at the trial). Systems: trust versus leverage as explicit alternatives. Reward: Act III trial modifier either way.

**6. Beall's Bottle** [v1.0]
Hook: the constable's hands shake by noon. Beats: a quiet chain of small kindnesses across the acts, covering his lapses, getting him fed, standing with him at the fort fence at dusk while he almost says what he saw. No single heroic moment; the quest is attendance. If completed before the trial, Beall testifies sober and believed. If completed at Rank 1+, his SEEN response opens fully. Systems: trust as accumulation, schedules. Reward: the game's most reliable witness.

**7. The Chainman's Widow** [v1.0]
Hook: Tam Hollis's sister arrives from Hagerstown asking after his effects. The player likely holds his cache: savings and the letter. Beats: none, really. Hand it over complete, skim it, or lie. She cannot check. The gossip network can. Systems: the trust economy demonstrated at its most naked. Reward: nothing material for honesty, which is the point; her testimony about the GENTLEMAN letter at trial if treated well.

**8. Benchmark** [v1.0]
Hook: Feig mentions that Gantt's predecessor, old surveyor Aldous Crane (no relation to the reverend, a coincidence the town enjoys), set brass benchmarks across the county, and that Gantt seemed oddly eager to buy them up as scrap. Beats: a county-wide hunt for five surviving benchmarks using the predecessor's field notes, terrain reading, and the surveyor's compass. Pure exploration, no combat, the game's love letter to letterbox-and-cache hunting. Finding all five lets the player independently re-derive the true plat line: alternate physical evidence for the trial that requires no witness at all. Systems: WOODSMAN, exploration, the evidence route. Reward: compass upgrade from Feig, and the most Gantt-proof evidence in the game.

## The Kirk

**9. The Collection Plate** [v1.0]
Hook: the revival's funds vanish and Crane, image cracking, needs it solved quietly. Beats: the thief is the Hartsell family, farm folk one bad season from Fenwick's list, who took it to pay a debt to Cresap of all people. Resolution: expose them (Kirk rises, something in the town dies a little), arrange quiet restitution (TONGUE and coin), or show Crane the arithmetic of his own collection against their debt and watch what he does with it. His choice, not the player's, and it foreshadows whether he is recruitable in Act III. Systems: faction texture, NPC moral agency. Reward: Kirk standing, Crane trust.

## The Hills

**10. Sign Painter** [v1.0]
Hook: Brahm observes that the losses map to the missing hex signs, and her hands are no longer steady enough for the work. Beats: restore four farm signs, each farm a small vignette (the Trents' guilt, a family that wants payment to allow it, a farm where the sign was removed by a grieving widower angry at God, handled gently). Night phenomena measurably recede from completed farms. Systems: warding craft, the fear meter made local and fixable. Reward: hex recipes, Hills standing, and the county visibly calmer, one farm at a time.

**11. Wrong-Sized Traps** [post]
Hook: Vachon's high-hollow trapline needs checking; she does not say why she will not check it herself. Beats: the line, the fog, one sprung trap holding nothing but a fistful of coarse hair, and a den at the end that resolves, deliberately, as a catamount: real, dangerous, and entirely natural. The quest is a control group for the player's paranoia, and Vachon knew it. What she actually wanted checked was her own nerve. Systems: WOODSMAN, tension without payoff as a design statement. Reward: TRAPS keyword, her trust, and a fine catamount-scare story for the Blue Mule.

**12. Bright's Lost Man** [post]
Hook: Bright pays double for a Braddock's Road run and drinks before it, not after. Beats: retrace the old incident west of the Narrows; find the remains and the truth, which is mundane and terrible: a storm, an injured man, a captain's arithmetic about the many and the one. Bring Bright peace, hold it over him, or (Rank 3) let the lost man speak his forgiveness or refuse it. Systems: SIGHT-variant resolutions, trust. Reward: Bright's true ROAD stories, the epilogue wagon offered free.

## The Uncanny

**13. The Cabin Kept** [v1.0]
Hook: Washington's cabin is always swept, never entered, and nobody is paid to do it. Beats: a stakeout quest with no antagonist. Watch a full night. Nothing comes. The broom has moved by morning. At Rank 2, one murmur, in the oldest voice in the game, still on duty. The quest never explains further and never should. Systems: atmosphere, restraint. Reward: the cabin becomes a safe rest spot flagged on the map, and the player knows one true quiet thing about the town.

**14. The Anatomy List** [v1.0]
Hook: Fenwick pays well for specimens, and the early jobs are merely ghoulish (Town risk, Road coin, Coombs's cheerful complicity while he lives). Beats: the third procurement request names Pelham Rood, who is not dead. The quest pivots on the spot: warn Rood (directly improving his Beat 2.4 survival), string Fenwick along to learn who placed the order (it traces, through two cutouts, to Gantt, the game's earliest hard link if the player is sharp), or take the money and live with the epilogue that follows. Systems: the main plot's back door, moral throughput. Reward: varies wildly by branch; the warning branch is the single highest-leverage side quest in the game.

**15. Wisp Ledger** [v1.0]
Hook: not a quest so much as a system with a name. Every wisp followed at Rank 1+ resolves from a seeded table: caches, snares, graves, and exactly one, near the Leap, that leads somewhere the Act IV descent can shortcut. Players who chase every light learn the table's shape and start reading wisps like weather. Systems: SIGHT risk and reward tuning. Reward: distributed.

**16. The Tinker's Route** [post]
Hook: the tinker's cart is stolen, which in a town this size is less a crime than a puzzle. Beats: track the cart (schedule knowledge, WOODSMAN), find it repainted at a shanty, and discover the thief wanted the cart, not the goods: a man building a leaving-life for a family that will not fit in a satchel. Resolution mirrors the Collection Plate: law, mercy, or arrangement. Systems: light, schedule literacy. Reward: the reflector upgrade at cost, and the tinker thereafter stocks one odd item per week.

## Burden Quests (one each, fires from the Prologue hook)

**Debt.** The creditor's agent arrives in Act II with legal paper and no malice, just arithmetic. Pay, flee across the ferry (he cannot follow, but the debt outlives the game), or discover the debt was sold to Cresap, folding it into the trial's stakes. [v1.0]

**Warrant.** A manhunter recognizes the player in the Blue Mule and, in the game's neatest mirror, Isaiah Freeman recognizes the manhunter. What the player did for or against Freeman decides what happens next without a single new mechanic. [v1.0]

**Second Sight.** Brahm knows the player's family name and the grandmother who had it before them, and her ritual costs the player less, because the door was never fully shut. A quiet quest about inheritance. [v1.0]

**The Letter.** The dead sibling's unfinished letter is addressed to the Lamar house. The sibling was the chainman on the survey two years back, the one working the day Mr. Lamar died. The final page is in Mrs. Lamar's keeping, and she will trade it only for her day of lucidity. The Burden that walks straight into the main plot's heart. [v1.0]

## Design Notes

- **Every faction gets one quest that is really about its best NPC's soul** (Beall, Crane, Vachon, Kent). The factions are people wearing crowds.
- **Three quests feed the trial** (Benchmark, The Sealed Record, The Chainman's Widow) so a thorough player walks into Act III overdetermined, which is how detective fiction rewards diligence.
- **One quest (Wrong-Sized Traps) pays off as nothing supernatural at all,** keeping Rank 2+ players honest about the difference between the Listening and the imagining.
- **No Questions is the game's conscience** and is written, budgeted, and tested with that weight.
