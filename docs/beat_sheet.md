# CAIUCTUCUC
## Main Quest Beat Sheet

**Companion to Design Document v0.3.0**
Beat Sheet v0.1.0
Author: M.B. Parks
Date: July 2026

Spoiler summary, stated up front so every beat below makes sense:

The phenomena are real. The thing under Wills Mountain is waking because McTeague's quarry crew dug past the platted line into a seam the Fort Cumberland garrison cut into and sealed in 1755. But the murders are human. Prosper Gantt falsified the plat that put the quarry on the mountain, and he has been killing to keep it hidden, using the town's growing terror of the curse as his cover. The mystery has two answers, one mundane and one not, and the game is honest about both. A Rank 0 player can catch Gantt and never learn what he accidentally uncovered. A Rank 3 player learns everything and then discovers that knowing is not proving.

Names and quest flags below reference the roster in Design Document Section 11.4.

---

## Prologue: The Road In (30 to 45 minutes)

**P1. Arrival.** The player walks into Cumberland with a wagon train at dusk. Tutorializes movement, keyword dialog with Captain Hosea Bright, and the satchel. The player's Burden fires its opening hook (a creditor's letter, a glimpsed wanted bill, a flash of the Glimmer, the sibling's letter naming a Cumberland address).

**P2. The Drover.** A drover in the train collapses and dies in the market square, natural causes, witnessed by half the town. His cur has nowhere to go. This is the dog vignette, and it quietly establishes the game's rule that death is ordinary here. Peg Doyle offers the player a bed at the Blue Mule and the first paying jobs board.

**P3. First Night.** Scripted fog. The player sees a light moving on Wills Mountain that no NPC will acknowledge seeing. At Rank 0 it reads as a lantern. With the second sight Burden it visibly is not one. No action possible, tone only.

## Act I: The Drowned Man (main flag: LEDGER)

**1.1. The Body.** Three days in (or immediately, if the player sleeps twice), a quarryman named Tam Hollis is found drowned in Wills Creek, upstream of the quarry, lungs full of water, boots dry. Magistrate Cresap wants it ruled an accident before the wagon season is disrupted. Constable Beall does not believe it and quietly hires the player as unofficial legs, since Beall's own credibility is soaked in brandy. Alternative entry: a player with the warrant Burden gets pressed into it by Cresap as leverage instead.

**1.2. Three Threads Open.** The investigation seeds three parallel lines the player can pull in any order:

- **The quarry.** McTeague is defensive. His men will not work the deep cut. High trust or a shared drink gets the phrase that launches the whole game: the rock was singing, and Tam went to listen. WOODSMAN tracking at the deep cut finds a second set of bootprints, human, unremarkable, leaving calmly.
- **The plat.** Tam's effects include a survey chain and a scrap copy of a plat line that does not match the courthouse record. Pelham Rood, if trusted, confirms the courthouse plat has been re-inked. This thread points at Gantt long before anything can be proven.
- **The fort.** Tam had been sleeping in the ruins to save wages. Sleeping there yourself is the Rank 1 SIGHT threshold. At Rank 0 the fort thread yields the fort ledger pages and Rood's discovery of the 1755 erasures. At Rank 1 it also yields a wisp that leads to Tam's hidden cache: his savings and a letter to his sister saying he had found something worth money, and that a gentleman had offered to buy his silence.

**1.3. The Gossip Turn.** Whichever thread the player pulls hardest, Peg Doyle notices and sells the observation. Brother Jubal Crane preaches his first sermon naming the drowning a judgment, and the Kirk faction meter goes live. From here on, faction reputation shifts with every public move the player makes on the case.

**1.4. Act Break: The Second Body.** Ezra Coombs the gravedigger is found dead at the bottom of Tam's own fresh grave, neck broken, ruled a fall. He was not investigating anything. He was robbing graves for Fenwick, and he opened the wrong coffin: the one where Gantt hid the original plat stone rubbings. At Rank 0 this reads as grim coincidence. At Rank 2 (if reached later, the murmur here retroactively fires) the grave murmurs a single word: PAPER. The town's fear becomes general. Act II begins.

## Act II: The Singing Rock (main flag: SEAM)

**2.1. Two Investigations Diverge.** The case is now officially two things at once, and the structure makes the player feel it:

- The **human line**: who profits, who lies, who was the gentleman in Tam's letter. Suspects the game deliberately keeps plausible: Gantt (the real killer), Fenwick (whose procurement list, stolen or charmed out of him, includes a name not yet dead: Pelham Rood's), Pyle (skimming, acting guilty about the wrong crime), and McTeague (who ordered the illegal dig and knows it).
- The **other line**: what is in the mountain. The fort ledger erasures, Mrs. Lamar's sedation, the hex sign pattern on the farms (every farm with losses had removed or painted over its sign, Josiah Trent's mill first), and Old Keys, who will not discuss the mountain at all until the player's Hills reputation and personal trust both clear a bar.

**2.2. The Widow's Offer.** Widow Brahm, approached with any warding question, offers the Rank 2 ritual, with its cost stated plainly. This is the game's central optional door. Players who decline can still finish Act II on the human line alone.

**2.3. The Lamar House.** The player gets into Mrs. Charity Lamar's house (invitation via trust, or burglary via hue and cry risk). Off laudanum for a day, she is lucid and terrifying: she has been at Rank 3 involuntarily since her husband died at the quarry two years ago, the event that made Dr. Ward start dosing her. She gives the player the **Winona token**, her husband's find from the deep cut, and one sentence: the girl who leapt was not fleeing a suitor, she was leading it away from the town. The Lovers Leap legend inverts. At Rank 2+, the token warms near the Narrows.

**2.4. Midpoint Turn: The Third Death, Interrupted.** Pelham Rood is attacked in the courthouse records room at night. If the player has previously warned Rood (trust) or is nearby (schedule knowledge), Rood survives and the game's back half changes texture: a living witness who saw nothing useful but is now scared, cooperative, and hunted. If not, Rood dies with the re-inked plat page in his hand, and at Rank 2 his murmur gives the game's clearest clue: HE MEASURES. Either way, the player now knows the killer is protecting the plat fraud specifically, narrowing suspects to Gantt and McTeague.

**2.5. The Revival Crests.** Crane holds a torchlit revival at the fort ruins. Attending offers three plays: let it happen (Kirk rises, town panic worsens), publicly debunk him (TONGUE challenge, Kirk falls, Town rises, and Crane, humiliated, privately confesses to the player what he actually saw on the mountain the night he arrived, worth a full SIGHT breadcrumb even at Rank 0), or quietly recruit him (a terrified fraud makes a surprisingly useful ally in Act III). During the revival, whatever the player chose, the ground under the fort audibly groans. Every NPC present hears it. Deniability ends. Act III begins.

## Act III: Hue and Cry (main flag: WARRANT)

**3.1. The Town Chooses Fear.** Militia patrols at night. Strangers and Hills folk harassed. Old Keys is nearly lynched on suspicion, and the player's intervention or absence here is the single largest Hills trust event in the game. Cresap, pressured, needs a culprit more than a truth.

**3.2. Naming the Killer.** The player can now assemble the case against Gantt through any of three routes, and this is where the systems braid:

- **Evidence route (any Rank).** Recover the plat stone rubbings from Coombs's effects (Fenwick has them, tradeable for protecting Rood or burying Fenwick's list), match them against the re-inked courthouse plat, and tie Gantt's boots to the deep cut prints via WOODSMAN. Solid enough for Judge Kent if the player's Town reputation and witness trust hold up at trial.
- **Testimony route (Rank 3).** Tam Hollis and, if he died, Rood, speak plainly. The player knows everything and can prove none of it. Converting ghost testimony into human action requires spending trust: Beall will plant himself where a ghost says to stand, but only for a player he believes. This is the trust problem the whole design has been aiming at.
- **Provocation route (any Rank, dangerous).** Let Gantt know what you have. He attempts to make the player the fourth victim at the Narrows overlook, a scripted ambush the player survives by combat, terrain escape, or (Storied) narrow rescue. Surviving it flips Beall to certainty regardless of trust, at the cost of Gantt now working in the open.

**3.3. Gantt's Endgame.** Cornered by any route, Gantt does the rational monstrous thing: he tells Cresap and Kent the full truth about the seam, the 1755 ledger, and what the garrison buried, and offers the town elders a deal. Bury the case, blame the deaths on the curse or on a convenient stranger (the player, if their hue and cry is high, or Old Keys, if the player never built Hills standing), and he will re-survey the line so the quarry, the town's whole economic future, stays legal. The elders waver. This beat lands differently depending on which faction owes the player: Town standing can force the trial, Road standing can produce Robey Marsh's testimony about who bought cave silence, Kirk standing (via a recruited Crane) can turn public sentiment overnight.

**3.4. The Trial or the Deal.** Either Gantt stands trial (verdict driven by evidence quality, witness trust, and Judge Kent, whose own sealed 1755-adjacent record the player may have found, making him either an ally in atonement or a compromised judge to be pressured) or the deal proceeds and Gantt walks, poisoning the mundane ending but not blocking the game. Both outcomes leave the same fact standing: the digging has not stopped, and the mountain has not settled. Act IV begins.

## Act IV: Under the Mountain (main flag: WINONA)

**4.1. The Convergence.** Livestock, then Nan Trent, go missing in one night. The cat, the mule, and every hex sign in the county point the same direction. Robey Marsh, sober for the first time in the game, offers the one thing only he has: the way in through the whiskey caves.

**4.2. The Descent.** The cave sequence, tuned by the Combat slider, is primarily traversal, warding craft (salt lines, hex work, the silvered blade if forged), and lantern management rather than fighting. Companions matter here: the dog tracks Nan, the raven scouts, the cat simply walks ahead as if it has been here before, because it has. At Rank 0 the descent plays as a rescue in a strange cave. At Rank 2+ the walls murmur in the voices of the 1755 garrison. At Rank 3, Corporal Pryor walks with you, and his daybook completes: the garrison cut the seam by accident, lost eleven men to something they refused to record, and sealed it with the help of a Shawnee woman the ledger calls only Winona, who stayed behind.

**4.3. The Chamber.** Nan is found unharmed, listening. What the player faces here, and what it appears to be, scales with SIGHT rank, from a cold cathedral-dark void at Rank 0 to the full truth at Rank 4 (deliberately unwritten in this beat sheet; the design doc reserves it). The ending branch fires by rank and by standing:

- **The Ledger Closed (Rank 0 to 1).** Carry Nan out, dynamite or seal the cave with McTeague's guilt-ridden help, and let the town call it settled. Epilogue text differs sharply depending on whether Gantt hanged or walked. The final image: next spring, the quarry reopens, one cut to the north.
- **The Buried Truth (Rank 2).** The player understands enough to choose a faction's answer: bury it properly with rites (Hills), consecrate and control it (Kirk, and Crane's redemption or final grift), or entomb it under engineered stone and civic silence (Town). Each is stable. None is permanent.
- **The Parley Kept (Rank 3).** With Pryor's testimony and Old Keys presiding, the player speaks the elder's true name and Winona's, and renegotiates the 1755 seal as it was meant to be kept: honored, remembered, and left alone. Requires high trust with both Old Keys and the dead. The one ending where the town, told nothing, is protected by people it will never thank.
- **The Opened Eye (Rank 4).** The player looks. What follows is triumph or ruin based on accumulated choices, and the player carries the social costs forever after in the epilogue's free roam.

**4.4. Epilogue: The Road Out, or Not.** Free roam resumes with the world state updated: verdicts, survivors, faction outcomes, Marks, and pet reactions all reflected in ambient dialog. Captain Bright's wagon train is loading for the Ohio country. The player can leave with it, a formal end screen, or stay, and the game does not mind which.

## Structural Notes

- **Every act break is a body or a near-body,** keeping the crime story spine taut even for players ignoring the supernatural entirely.
- **Gantt is catchable in Act III at Rank 0.** The supernatural is never required for the mystery's human half, honoring design pillar 1.
- **Rank 2 is positioned as the game's emotional center** (the Widow's cost, the Lamar house) rather than a power-up.
- **Trust is the currency of Act III, faction reputation the currency of Act IV.** The systems each get an act where they carry the plot.
- **Nan Trent is the finale's stakes** precisely because she is the roster's most powerless member, and every companion animal has been pointing at her house since Act II for players watching.
- **Estimated main path length:** 8 to 12 hours, with the full four-thread completionist run near 20.
