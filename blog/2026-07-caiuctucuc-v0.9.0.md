# CAIUCTUCUC v0.9.0: Who Walks Into Cumberland

*Draft for greenshoegarage.com, with release v0.9.0*

The last big gap is closed: the game finally asks who you are before it asks anything else.

Origin, trade, burden, three selects and a button, and every choice is mechanical the way the design doc demanded three documents and nine versions ago. A Scots-Irish drover walks in with the Road already halfway friendly. A German craftsman knows the word HEX before anyone teaches it. A freed tradesman knows PAPERS, and Isaiah Freeman knows him, and the manhunter mirror in Act II lands entirely differently because of it. The gunsmith's flintlock arrives Sound with two loads of shot. The preacher walks in wearing the black, which is to say, walks in wearing a disguise the hue and cry has never seen.

And the burdens are real weights. Debt starts you with two silver and a factor who eventually arrives with legal paper and no malice, just arithmetic. The warrant primes Magistrate Cresap's most dangerous conversation from minute one, and puts a traveler in the Blue Mule who has been looking at you for two days. Second sight opens you at the Glimmer from birth and, in the release's quietest touch, discounts Brahm's ritual, because for you the door was never fully shut, and she recognizes your grandmother's eyes. The unfinished letter is addressed to the Lamar house, and the woman at the window, lucid for one hard-bought hour, will trade its final page for nothing but the truth of why you came, and hands you the Winona token with it.

For the stranger-proofing the roadmap demanded, the journal now opens with THE TRAIL: one plain sentence about where the case wants you next, from Baltimore Street runs east on the first evening all the way to the wagons load whenever you are ready, or never. Every stage of the mystery answers, and a test walks the whole chain to prove no stage shrugs.

And the sound grew ears. Indoors muffles, the caves breathe low and close, and on the north road there is a slow wooden thump on the wind, fading with distance: the Trent mill, turning. It has been turning since the game began. In the last act, you already know, it stops, and now stopping is something you hear.

One hundred three tests. Next release is the one with a 1 in front of it.
