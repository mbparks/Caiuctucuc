# CAIUCTUCUC v0.1.0: Three Ways Out

*Draft for greenshoegarage.com, with release v0.1.0*

The slice is complete, which means the theft on Baltimore Street can now be survived three different ways, and each one says something about the game this is going to be.

You can pay Peg for the corner room and let six hours pass. You can simply walk it off, because small towns cool the way small towns do, slowly and with looks. Or, new tonight, you can duck behind the market to the coat rack and shrug into the preacher's black, because the hue and cry remembers what the witnesses saw, not who you are. Wear the coat you stole in and Beall comes for you at a warrant stride. Wear a different one and the street drops to muttering, since nobody can quite swear to your face. The disguise math is honest: a new coat buys you exactly one level of doubt, and never washes you cleaner than gossip while the heat is real.

The town keeps hours now too. There is a clock in the HUD, dusk purples the street, night blues it, and the wind (procedurally generated, no samples, just brown noise through a lowpass) blows harder after dark. Peg pours from six in the morning until midnight and sleeps like a working woman. Beall stands his post from eight to eight, takes his pint at the Mule until midnight, and goes home. If you want the constable, you now need to know when to want him.

And if he corners you, a new option: press Q and put your hands out. Three silver instead of five, ten hours in the gaol, and one quiet point of the constable's respect, because Beall remembers a man who comes quiet. That trust point is the first one the game has ever awarded, and it is earned by giving up. I like what that says.

Old saves migrate automatically. Thirty three tests, all green. Next milestone: the whole town.
