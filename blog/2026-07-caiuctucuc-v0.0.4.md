# CAIUCTUCUC v0.0.4: One Street, One Crime, One Way Out

*Draft for greenshoegarage.com, with release v0.0.4*

The vertical slice is playable. Baltimore Street exists: the Blue Mule on the north side, the market at its shoulder, the courthouse block down the way, and Constable Amos Beall standing in front of it like he grew there.

Here is the whole game in one minute. There is a purse on the market stall. Take it and six silver is yours, and the HUD in the corner ticks from QUIET straight to CONSTABLE, because you did it in the open market like an amateur, and the word moves faster than you do. Beall comes off his post and he does not stop. He slides along walls, he corners, and if he collars you it is a five silver fine and a walk back from the courthouse with your hat askew.

Or: duck into the Blue Mule, pay Peg one silver for the corner room, and let six hours pass. When you come out the street has cooled from CONSTABLE to GOSSIP, which means sharp looks and short answers instead of a man with a warrant stride. Time also cools heat on its own if you just walk it off, slowly, the way small towns forget slowly.

Talk to Beall before you rob the market, though. Ask him about the FORT and count the seconds before his hand finds his flask pocket. There is a word that would open him up, but he is right that some questions want a warmer acquaintance.

Twenty seven tests green, including one that proves a night at the Mule drops the law exactly one level, and one that proves Beall cannot walk through walls, only around them.

The loop is real now: crime, heat, pursuit, refuge. Everything from here is more town, more voices, and eventually, something under the mountain.
