# CAIUCTUCUC v0.4.0: Pockets and Workbenches

*Draft for greenshoegarage.com, with release v0.4.0*

The game has things now. Sixty of them, the whole item list from the design doc, and every one is usable, tradeable, or readable, because that was the rule and rules are cheap until you enforce them in a registry.

Press I and the satchel opens: sixteen slots, no weight math, no grid puzzles. Consumables stack. Tools and weapons do not, because each one carries its own condition: Sound, Worn, or Broke, with the numbers hidden the way the design demanded. A flintlock left out in metaphorical rain wears twice as fast. Broke means unusable, never destroyed, archive not delete, even for firearms.

Repair comes in two honesties. In the field, with a whetstone and an oil rag, you can patch a Broke thing back to Worn, and your HANDS will someday determine how deep in the Worn band it lands, but the field never reaches Sound. Sound requires Feig, his counter, his fee, and the specific grunt he gives your gear before he makes it right. The wilderness can limp home. It cannot stay pristine. That asymmetry is the whole repair design in one sentence.

The workbenches are real too: the hearth at the Blue Mule makes stew, poultices, and tallow candles; Ward's bench compounds tonics and lays salt lines; and Feig's anvil will cast a silver dollar into three loads of shot, which I consider the most Cumberland sentence in the game so far. Recipes are two ingredients at the most, shown plainly, no crafting experience bar, nothing to grind.

The store has a counter with prices, and selling fetches half, except whiskey and silver dollars, which fetch full, because on this frontier they are not goods, they are money. There is a chest in your corner room for the stash. And the coat system quietly grew up: coats are items now, equipping the preacher's black changes your sprite and your disguise in the same motion, because the wardrobe and the alibi were always the same system waiting to meet.

Fifty five tests. My favorite failure this release: the stew test complained you could cook with half the makings, and the bug was in the test, because the starting kit already carries two johnnycakes. The game was right. The pot knew.
