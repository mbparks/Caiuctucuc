# CAIUCTUCUC v0.6.0: The Glimmer and the Grave

*Draft for greenshoegarage.com, with release v0.6.0*

This is the release where the game asks if you are sure.

Sleep rough in the fort ruins and a panel opens with the question the whole design was built around: something at the edge of sleep waits to be let in, and it will not close again once opened. Say yes and you wake at Rank 1, the Glimmer, and the swamp gas over the glacis resolves into three pale lights that were always there, each holding to the seeded ledger the design promised: follow one and it sinks over a buried tin of silver, or it goes out an arm's length ahead of ground that was never there. Even odds, decided by the light's own name, forever. Say no and you sleep plainly, and the game respects the no. The skeptic run is protected by a test now: Act I completes at Rank 0 with the other world entirely untouched.

Rank 2 lives with Widow Brahm, who finally moved into her cottage north of the courthouse and talks to no one, as the gossip graph has always insisted. Bring her two bundles of dried sassafras, because you gather, you carry, you keep your word, and that is most of warding and half of everything else, and she will speak of the Listening. The price is stated plainly and paid in advance: a night of your memory gone entire, one point off your ceiling, and something of hers that does not grow back. Take it, and stand at Coombs's grave, and one word comes up in his gap-toothed voice, patient as a man with all the time there is: PAPER.

Dying is in the game now too, since the creek is colder than it looks and some lights are snares. Death is never a reload. You wake at Ward's surgery a day later and a few silver lighter, carrying a Mark from the pool: a limp, a tremor, a white streak, a scar, an ear that rings near cold spots. Five at most, no repeats, and the first crossing surfaces a word, CROSSED, that opens a new conversation at the widow's gate. On Storied you cannot die at all, and the test suite holds Storied to that promise. On Perilous the crossing fee doubles. The three sliders are live in the menu, changeable anytime, no penalty, exactly as designed.

Hex signs and salt lines place from the satchel now, and a snare that lunges across a ward line breaks like water on a stone. At Rank 2 the wards visibly glow.

Seventy six tests. The suite now guarantees, among other things, that Brahm never sells the door to a stranger, that Marks never repeat, and that the wisp table keeps its balance. Next: Acts II and III, where knowing who the killer is stops being the hard part.
