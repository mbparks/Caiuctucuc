# CAIUCTUCUC v0.5.0: The Drowned Man

*Draft for greenshoegarage.com, with release v0.5.0*

The game has a mystery now, which is to say the game is finally the game.

Walk into the market on your first evening and a drover ahead of you folds mid-sentence, dead before he lands, natural as weather. Half the town sees it. His cur sits down beside him and does not move. That is the prologue teaching you the rule the whole design leans on: death is ordinary here. What comes next is not.

Next morning, shouting from the creek. Tam Hollis, quarryman, drowned upstream of the workings with his lungs full and his boots dry, and Constable Beall, whose credibility is soaked in brandy and knows it, steers you off the street by the elbow and asks for your legs. From there the case opens three ways and any one of them will carry you to the act break. Climb the quarry road and find the second set of bootprints at the deep cut, calm and even and leaving; earn McTeague enough to hear about the singing. Or take the paper trail: Tam's effects at Ward's surgery hold a plat scrap, and the courthouse plat book, laid against it, has been re-inked by a careful hand. Or find where Tam slept in the fort ruins, and under a loose hearthstone, his savings and a letter about a gentleman who offered to buy his silence.

Pull any thread taut and the game tells you to sleep on it. Do, and the morning after belongs to Ezra Coombs, dead at the bottom of Tam's own fresh grave, ruled a fall, and the gravedigger's sprite is simply gone from the churchyard schedule, because the case engine removed him from the world. The journal keeps all of it now, grouped by thread, the case file the design always promised.

Also in this release: press backtick. A prompt opens that answers to exactly four words, SNAKEOIL, SPECIE, PARDON, and LANTERNS, and one of them quietly sets a flag called snakeOilSalesman that will matter in an ending someday. And the twelve townsfolk finally stopped being twins: every named NPC wears their own coat and hat colors now, so Peg's oxblood is not Fenwick's too-good plum is not Ward's surgical green.

Sixty eight tests, including three that each complete Act I through a single thread with the other two untouched, which was the milestone's whole promise: the mystery is solvable any way you come at it.

Next on the roadmap: the Glimmer, the graves, and what dying leaves on you.
