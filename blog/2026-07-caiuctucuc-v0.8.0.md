# CAIUCTUCUC v0.8.0: Under the Mountain

*Draft for greenshoegarage.com, with release v0.8.0*

The mill wheel stops. That is how the last act announces itself: livestock in the night, then Nan Trent gone from a locked room, and every dog, cat, and mule in the county looking at the mountain. Robey Marsh, stone sober for the first time in living memory, nails a horseshoe over the cave mouth on the quarry road. The way in, marked.

The caves are the game's one long dark: switchbacks and side pockets, a cold pool, two pale lights keeping their seeded ledger down where nobody will find your tin, Marsh's cellar of cave-aged casks behind his horseshoe at the fork, and the lantern's dithered circle the only geometry you own. If the cat is at your heel, it is not at your heel down here. It walks ahead. It has been here before. That single behavior, the cat leading in the dark, was an exit criterion written into the roadmap months of versions ago, and it is in, and it is exactly as unsettling as I hoped.

At the bottom: a cold cathedral dark, the singing very quiet now, like a thing aware it is being visited, and Nan sitting at the center, unharmed, listening. One chamber, two endings, and the same save can reach both. Carry her out and seal the cave, and the town calls it settled, and next spring the quarry reopens one cut to the north. Or, if you paid Brahm's price and opened the second door, bury it properly, with rites older than the county, and walk Nan out by the hand into an epilogue the town will never understand it owes you. The Buried Truth is gated on the Listening and nothing else, because the ending was never about items. It was about whether you chose to hear.

The companions arrived too. The drover's cur from the very first scene finally gets its vignette: it looks at the place its drover fell, then at you, and makes the only decision left to it. The dog growls at lights you have not seen yet. The fort cat files its adoption paperwork retroactively and keeps the rats and worse at distance.

And the crossing. If the Road trusts you and you have asked Shanks the right word at night, there is a family at the ferry landing: Ruth, Samuel, and a baby they have not named yet, in case. There is no coin at the end of it and no joke anywhere in it. You walk them through the lanes you know, or you draw the rider off yourself and wear the heat home, and nobody says thank you out loud, and nobody has to. It is one small quest and it is the game's conscience, and the departure screen remembers it above almost everything else.

Ninety six tests. This release's bug was a good one: the town briefly had two layers named doors and the second quietly shadowed the first, sealing the cave mouth. The loader now refuses duplicate layer names forever, which is how a bug becomes a law.

Content complete. Next: the polish pass, the character creator, and then v1.0.
