# CAIUCTUCUC v0.2.0: Twelve Voices and a Town to Hold Them

*Draft for greenshoegarage.com, with release v0.2.0*

The town exists. Not a street, the town: Wills Creek down the west side with a bridge and a ford, Baltimore Street running east past the Blue Mule and the market to the kirk, Mechanic Street and Greene Street crossing it, the fort ruin brooding on the south hill, the ferry below that, and twenty two doors that all open. Every building on the plat map now has an inside.

Twelve people live here, and all twelve talk. Ask Feig the smith about IRON once he trusts you and hear about the cold iron blade he forged for a man who died inside the season. Ask McTeague about the SINGING and watch a foreman built like a rockslide pull you out of the wind before he answers. Ask Gantt the surveyor about the PLAT and receive the most reasonable answer in the world from the most reasonable man in town, and notice that his smile does not move while his eyes do. Everyone keeps hours. The gravedigger works nights. The doctor barely sleeps. If you want a particular person, learn their day.

The town also talks about you. Steal from the market and the rumor enters a network with real edges and real delays: Beall hears in an hour, Peg hears in five, because Beall drinks at the Mule and Peg is Peg, and the magistrate knows by suppertime tomorrow. Walk into the tavern after and she does not look up: word about the market got here before you did, friend. The widow Brahm, when she arrives, will hear nothing through this network, because she talks to no one, and the graph literally has no edges from her. I am unreasonably pleased that her silence is enforced by data structure.

There is a jobs board now, two honest livings and one that is not, and the dishonest one is only dishonest if you do it in daylight, because daylight has eyes. There is a journal on J that shows your words, your standing, your confidences, and the hour. Trust has become arithmetic: kept promises count one, a caught lie costs two, and the first point anyone ever earns is still for surrendering quietly.

Forty one tests. The best one loads the whole town and proves that every door leads somewhere, every scheduled hour has a place to stand, and every one of the twelve has a voice. Next: the art pass, where the rectangles learn to be people.
