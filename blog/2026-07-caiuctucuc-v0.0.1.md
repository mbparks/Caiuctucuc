# Something Under the Mountain: Announcing CAIUCTUCUC

*Draft for greenshoegarage.com / gearsofresistance.com, with release v0.0.1*

I have started building a game set two blocks from where I am sitting, two hundred and twenty six years ago.

CAIUCTUCUC (the Shawnee name for the village at the Wills Creek confluence, and yes, I will be pronouncing it differently every time) is a top down, open world supernatural mystery set in Cumberland, Maryland, in 1800. The fort is a ruin. The National Road does not exist yet. The town is taverns, wagon trains, and fog off the Potomac, and something under Wills Mountain has noticed that the new quarry is digging in its direction.

The design targets are Ultima and Grand Theft Auto, translated into period furniture. Keyword conversation instead of dialog trees. A hue and cry system instead of a wanted meter: gossip, then the constable, then a militia muster, then a posted bounty, and if it gets too hot you can always ford the Potomac, because Maryland constables have no jurisdiction on the Virginia shore. Horses instead of cars. Whiskey as currency, bribe material, and firestarter, all at once.

The part I am proudest of on paper is called SIGHT. The mystery is fully solvable as a pure crime story by a skeptic who never believes a word of the ghost business. Or you can sleep in the fort ruins, learn a ritual from a hexerei widow at a cost she pays too, and start hearing what the graves have to say. Every rank of it is a threshold you cross on purpose, and the game asks if you are sure. It is a horror dial you turn yourself.

v0.0.1 is a walking skeleton: a fixed step loop, a little fellow in a red hat crossing a placeholder creek at a placeholder ford, saves that export to JSON, and a test harness that already knows things like the fact that crossing the river decays your heat six times faster. The whole paper design (design doc, beat sheet, town layout, keyword matrix, side quests, and a map drawn as an 1800 surveyor's plat with the plot's central fraud hidden on it in red ink) ships in the repo under docs.

GPL-3.0, browser based, local first, built in the open like everything else here. Make. Hack. Learn. Share. Repeat.
